The 19th annual Funtastik Summer Classic welcomes you to join in the fun and competition this Labor Day weekend. This competition is held in conjuction with a huge three day Kipona (sparkling river) festival. Circle kickers, novice, intermediate and pro kickers all have a great time playing, competing, meeting new friends and busting new moves. If you are new to footbag and are on the East Coast, this tournament is a must to attend. Meet, watch and/or learn from several World Champions that always put on quite a show.

EVENTS

FOOTBAG NET-    The pool format is two out of 3 
games to 11 points.  Semi final & final games are 2 out of 3 games to 15 
points changing sides at 8 points.  Must win by two points in all 
games.  This format is subject to change at the sole discretion of the 
tournament director.  The pool captains are responsible for
their pool playing games in a timely fashion.  Delinquent pools could 
forfeit games.

FOOTBAG FREESTYLE-   Time limit is 2 minutes for singles
pros and 1:30 for amateurs.  Freestyle competitors might be 
required to judge.  Hand in your music and introduction card 
before the event.  Pick up music after the event.

FOOTBAG GOLF-   One 9 hole round of golf will be played that follows 
the standard footbag golf rules.  

CROSS COUNTRY GOLF- 9 hole round of golf that goes anywhere. $2 ENTRY FEE 
($5 open) Pays 2 places from fees collected.  Minimum of 10 to run event.

SKILLS CONTESTS- $7 ($10 if not entered in tournament)
Winner takes all basis.  2003 champions get a bye to finals  Congrats to:
  Iron Man-  Eric Wulff                     Freestyle Shred- Greg Nelson
  Service Poaching- Emanuel Bouchard        Super Man- Greg Nelson 

FREESTYLE SHRED- 45 sec. max, most adds win, no choreography, generic music.
	Rules- 1. Shred till you drop
	           2. 3 drops & you are done
	           3. Cannot do same trick more than 5 times in a row  
	           4. The top 3 make it to finals
	    Yes, your routine will be video taped to verify adds.

SERVICE POACHING- This is a two-person event.  Ten serves, thus 10 
attempts at service blocks.  Preliminary rounds- the most blocks win.  
Final round- highest accumulative score from judges win.
		1. Service block must occur at the net.
		2. Blocker must be airborne to count.
		3. The top 3 make it to finals.

IRON MAN- The best time wins.  Any drop in consecutive or toe delays, 
must restart from the beginning. 
1. 100 consecutive kicks, 10 toe delays, 1 
around the world with each foot, golf accuracy shots, 1 net service & 1 set 
& spike (airborne contact above net required)
2. Two footbags allowed. 
3. Body contact is allowed during golf shots.
4. The top 3 make it to finals.

SUPER MAN- Completion of tasks in the best time wins.  40 adds, 50 
consecutives, net serve to gladiator until scoring a point, 
receive gladiator serve until scoring a point & 3 various golf shots done 
within 4 mins.
1. Seconds added to score equal to distance
2. Any golf shot made cancels out distance from longest miss.
3. Net footbag must be used during net portion.

FOUR SQUARE- winner takes all  $2 to enter ($5 if not entered in tournament) 
max. 16 players, random court drawing- 4 courts, standard four square 
rules.  Top player from each square goes to final round.
