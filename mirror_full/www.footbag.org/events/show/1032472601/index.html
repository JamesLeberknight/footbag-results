Introduction
The Chilly Philly is now in its third year.  Borrowing from the 
tradition of the long beloved East Must jams, we gather each year around 
New Year's day to start the year off right - that is, we start out the New 
Year like we ended the last...  Shredding!

The Chilly Philly has been fairly informal in past years, and will again be 
in 
this format this year.  However, backing for freestyle in the Phildelphia 
area has grown leaps and bounds in the past year and a half.  We have 
more young players, more and better veteran players.  We're surrounded 
by states filled with motivated players willing to travel, and the community 
as a whole is more open to supporting our sport than ever before.

That said, we're going to make this year's Chilly Philly a little more fun 
for 
the players.  Our venue is the Adam's Mark Hotel, 
and it is spacious and well lit with great traction...  This venue also is 
2 miles from one of the hippest main street areas in Philadelphia 
and is readily accessible by train or car or plane.  There will be player 
packsthis year for an extremely modest price (includes a Chilly Philly 
long sleave T with original artwork by a local artist), and we'll also be 
trying to get as many players together for a player's party after the fact 
(players under 21 will need to have a parent or guardian present). 

Last year we had over 50 players in attendance, including some 
of the most dominant players in the game.  We're shooting to demolish 
that record this year.  Only you can help us do that.  Mark your calendar, 
start making your plans, practice.

Competition/Demo Format
The host venue would like us to show footbag to their members.  We'd 
like to have a competition portion of the evening.  To kill two birds with 
one stone, we're going to hold our competition as the demo.  Here's how 
it will work.

Open level players are going to compete first so that we show our best 
talent while the most spectators are around to watch.  Open level players 
should arrive at 5:00 p.m. to warm up as the competition will begin 
promptly at 5:30.  After the open players, we'll have the intermediates and 
novice players.  PLEASE READ THE SCORING INFORMATION BELOW IF 
YOU PLAN ON COMPETING.  PLEASE READ PRIOR TO EMAILING ME 
QUESTIONS.

Open players - Shred 30.  30 seconds or two drops.  To provide "real 
time" feedback to the crowd, we are going to release unofficial scores 
after each competitor finishes.  The crowd will not understand "Uniques" 
and scoring adjustments, so we'll count adds as we go and announce 
those scores after each player finishes.  We will be video taping the open 
level players and will perform a complete scoring analysis after the fact to 
determine the true champion.  Same scoring methods as usual... 
Uniques, adds, drops all figure in.  raw add count + (unique tricks >= 3 
adds * add ratio)

Intermediate.  Shred 30.  Most adds in 30 seconds.  We want to 
encourage the intermediate level players to keep the ball off the floor - 
and the best way to win this event is to keep the ball up (drops don't 
count as tricks and they waste time, so they hurt you a lot).  That said, 
we'll NOT be factoring in unique tricks nearly as heavily as for the open 
players.  Only if two players are dead even in scoring will we resort to 
considering uniques.  In other words, intermediate scoring will be based 
solely upon total adds performed in 30 seconds minus a substantial 
deduction for drops.  Your goal as an intermediate in this particular 
competition is to keep the ball up, and do your bread and butter tricks.

Novice.  Trick Builder 30.  This is a new event.  For novice players, we 
want to encourage originality and build your confidence.  Drops will not be 
considered in the scoring unless two players are dead even in the 
results.  Your job as an novice in this particular competition is to show us 
your variety of tricks.  Be creative.  Show us that you know some tricks on 
both sides of your body.  Show us your flyers.  Show us originality and 
style.  Don't worry about stringing tricks together like a pro - you're not a 
pro yet, so just show us what you've been practicing.  Novices will be 
judged at the sole discretion of our qualified judges (people like myself, 
Kenny Shults, Jon Schneider...) based on who we think the most stylish 
up and comer is.
