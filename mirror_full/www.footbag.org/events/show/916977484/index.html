This weekend the State Games of Oregon Footbag Tournament was witness to a 
strange phenomenon. As the pasta dinner started at Dennis and Becca's on 
Friday night the rain was just wrapping up its business. The next drop of 
rain to fall on Eugene was after the last good bye Sunday evening.  Seven 
years I have been directing footbag tournaments and this is the first time 
the rain gave a tournament the right-of-way.

Guffaws and sunny smiles spread across the Education Field at U of O, as 
forty legs soaked up rays and sent footbags flying. Tournament Directors 
Kendall KIC and Becca English-Ross wondered what happened to the gaggle of 
West Coast footbag players who usually show up to slosh about in the rain. 
Your name may have been mentioned, but your presence was not mourned because 
nothing was available to dampen the playful spirits leaping about campus.

Historically this tournament was in July at Mt. Hood Community College in Gresham. Attendance has been sparce and the dates conflicting so Free FLOE stepped in to save the event. Entry fee will be $30 for open and intermediate players; players pack will include a State Games of Oregon T-shirt for starters. Free entry to novice/beginners. The sport of Footbag will be highlighted at the State Games of Oregon Opening Ceremonies on July 9th at 6:00 p.m., Tualatin Hills Recreation Center in Tualatin, everyone is invited to march with Kenny Shults in the parade of athletes.
